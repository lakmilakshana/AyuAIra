LITERATURE SURVEY

The development of an Intelligent Ayurvedic Medicine System for Arthritis has gained significant attention in recent years. Several studies have investigated the potential benefits of Ayurvedic medicine in the treatment of arthritis, and researchers have begun exploring the integration of intelligent systems to enhance its efficacy. One notable study conducted at Yakkala Wickramarachchi University and Hospital assessed the effectiveness of Ayurvedic therapies, including herbal formulations and manual techniques, in reducing pain and improving joint function in patients with arthritis. The results showed promising outcomes, with a significant reduction in pain scores and improved mobility observed in the study participants. Similarly, another research conducted at Rajagiriya University and Hospital explored the use of intelligent systems based on Ayurvedic principles for the diagnosis and treatment of arthritis. The study utilized a dataset collected from a diverse population of arthritis patients and employed machine learning algorithms to develop a predictive model for personalized treatment recommendations. The findings demonstrated the potential of an Intelligent Ayurvedic Medicine System in optimizing treatment outcomes by tailoring therapies to individual patient needs. These studies highlight the growing interest in integrating Ayurvedic medicine with intelligent systems and the potential benefits of such an approach in managing arthritis effectively.

RESEARCH GAP

Previous research has explored various approaches for the detection and management of arthritis, including both traditional and modern methods. However, a research gap exists regarding the integration of advanced technology with traditional Ayurvedic medicine specifically for arthritis. While previous studies have focused on either modern medical interventions or traditional Ayurvedic treatments in isolation, there is limited research that combines both approaches into a comprehensive system. Therefore, there is a need for research to bridge this gap by developing and evaluating an intelligent Ayurvedic medicine system that effectively integrates advanced technology with traditional Ayurvedic knowledge for early detection and management of arthritis.

RESEARCH PROBLEM

The research problem in the development of an Intelligent Ayurvedic Medicine System for Arthritis lies in several key areas. Firstly, the lack of standardization within Ayurvedic medicine poses challenges in creating a system that can effectively diagnose and treat arthritis based on Ayurvedic principles. Secondly, the limited clinical evidence supporting the efficacy and safety of Ayurvedic medicine for arthritis calls for further rigorous research, including well-designed randomized controlled trials. Thirdly, the integration of personalized treatment approaches, considering an individual's unique constitution, into an intelligent system requires more exploration. Additionally, investigating the integration of Ayurvedic and modern medicine, as well as ensuring user acceptance and usability of the system, are critical areas that need to be addressed. By addressing these research gaps, the field of Intelligent Ayurvedic Medicine Systems for Arthritis can advance, providing a stronger evidence base and improved integration with conventional medicine.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

The objective of this research is to devise an advanced and intelligent Ayurvedic medicine system with the purpose of promptly identifying and efficiently managing arthritis. This will involve incorporating cutting-edge technologies such as machine learning, natural language processing, and data analysis to develop a comprehensive framework. The system will utilize patient data, symptom analysis, and historical treatment outcomes to generate personalized treatment plans. The ultimate goal is to enhance the early detection, prognosis, and management of arthritis using Ayurvedic principles and modern advancements in medical technology.

METHODOLOGY

AyuAIra is very effective at identifying and treating osteoarthritis, and rheumatoid arthritis. Additional investigation is required to assess AyuAIra's usefulness in treating different kinds of arthritis and to enhance the precision and efficacy of the system.
The application is consisting of four major components;
  • X-Ray Image Analyzer
  • Blood Report Monitoring
  • Ayurvedic Treatment Recommendation
  • Continious Monitoring and Feedback

X-Ray Image Analyzer

X-ray imaging is critical in identifying pain in the joints, especially in cases of arthritis. However, the traditional method of neurological specialists manually examining X-rays and relying on their experience in the Ayurvedic approach is time-consuming and error-prone. To address these constraints, there has been a substantial increase in the pursuit of automated approaches that use technology and machine learning algorithms to detect joint inflammation and quantify its severity using X-ray images.

Blood Report Monitoring

Blood tests are mainly used to monitor the progression of arthritis and evaluate treatment effectiveness. analyzing blood tests for arthritis face complex challenges due to the many factors that can influence the interpretation of values ESR, CRP, anti-CCP antibodies, and RF, including patient-specific and external factors, including age, sex, medication use, laboratory variation, and need to consider multiple values together with clinical history and presentation. Their analysis can be complex and time-consuming. This study aims to investigate the use of deep learning algorithms to analyze time-series data of blood test results and track the progression of arthritis and the effectiveness of ayurvedic treatments.

Ayurvedic Treatment Recommendation

The Ayurvedic Treatment Recommendation System is a research component to provide specific treatment recommendations for arthritis patients based on the principles of Ayurvedic medicine, considering the patient's age, sex, symptoms, and overall health. To identify the best algorithm for the Ayurvedic treatment recommendation system, several machine-learning models will be evaluated in this research. Several of the algorithms up for evaluation are the decision tree, random forest, logistic regression, support vector machine, and neural network. The performance metrics obtained through experimentation will be used to determine which algorithm is most appropriate for the Ayurvedic treatment recommendation system. Precision, recall, accuracy, and F1 score will all be used to evaluate the algorithms' effectiveness. As a result of the evaluation, the decision tree method was determined to be the best choice for this system.

Continuous Monitoring and Feedback

The continuous monitoring and feedback component of the Intelligent Ayurvedic Medicine System for Arthritis aims to develop a predictive model that considers the patient's symptoms, treatment progress, and overall health and provides real-time feedback to the treating doctor. This component is crucial for the proper treatment of arthritis since it allows for the early detection of changes in the patient's health, which may help with the avoidance of disease progression and the improvement of treatment plans. We provide an innovative approach in this methodology that use a combination of deep learning algorithms to integrate data from numerous sources, including physiological signals and self-reported data. Several machine learning models were experimented with to make this monitoring, including Artificial Neural Network (ANN), Decision Tree, Random Forest, XG boost, and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM). Based on the results of these algorithms, the XG Boost model was chosen as the most appropriate model for further implementations.
